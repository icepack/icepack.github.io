icepack.models.viscosity module¶

Functions for calculating the viscosity of a glacier

This module contains procedures for computing the viscosity of a glacier and, in particular, the viscous part of the action functional for ice flow. Several flow models all have essentially the same viscous part.

icepack.models.viscosity.rate_factor(T)[source]¶

Compute the rate factor in Glen’s flow law for a given temperature

The strain rate \(\dot\varepsilon\) of ice resulting from a stress \(\tau\) is

\[\dot\varepsilon = A(T)\tau^3\]

where \(A(T)\) is the temperature-dependent rate factor:

\[A(T) = A_0\exp(-Q/RT)\]

where \(R\) is the ideal gas constant, \(Q\) has units of energy per mole, and \(A_0\) is a prefactor with units of pressure \(\text{MPa}^{-3}\times\text{yr}^{-1}\).

icepack.models.viscosity.viscosity_depth_averaged(u, h, A)[source]¶

Return the viscous part of the action for depth-averaged models

The viscous component of the action for depth-averaged ice flow is

\[E(u) = \frac{n}{n+1}\int_\Omega h\cdot M(\dot\varepsilon, A):\dot\varepsilon\hspace{2pt} dx\]

where \(M(\dot\varepsilon, A)\) is the membrane stress tensor

\[M(\dot\varepsilon, A) = A^{-1/n}|\dot\varepsilon|^{1/n - 1} (\dot\varepsilon + \text{tr}\dot\varepsilon\cdot I).\]

This form assumes that we’re using the fluidity parameter instead the rheology parameter, the temperature, etc. To use a different variable, you can implement your own viscosity functional and pass it as an argument when initializing model objects to use your functional instead.

  • h (firedrake.Function) – ice thickness
  • A (firedrake.Function) – ice fluidity parameter
